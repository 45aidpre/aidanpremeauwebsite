Menu Randomizer

This was one of my initial programming projects, which I developed independently and further enhanced during my studies at Florida Gulf Coast University. While working in the culinary industry, I conceived the idea of practicing my C++ skills by creating a program that can take one or more lists of items and randomly generate a menu. So far, the project has been successful, but I aim to expand its capabilities by developing a website that can produce various menu styles and allow users to print their personalized menus.

Natural Computing Algorithms

In the "Intro to Natural Computing" course at Florida Gulf Coast University, I explored how natural phenomena inspire algorithms in coding and animation. The course covered various methods, including Boids algorithm, Lindenmayer Systems, and Cellular Automata, all of which simulate natural occurrences. For instance, the Boids algorithm simulates flocking behavior in birds by defining rules for individual particle movement and interactions, commonly used in animation and visual effects.
